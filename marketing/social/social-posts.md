## LinkedIn

**Title:** Failover ≠ Correctover — Why Your AI Gateway Needs Verified Failover

**Body:**
The uncomfortable truth about every major AI gateway today: they switch providers the moment HTTP 200 comes back. Not after verifying the response. Not after checking model identity. Not after validating cost.

This isn't failover — it's a false sense of security.

What happens when:
- The backup provider returns a different model than the one you requested?
- The response looks valid but is semantically wrong?
- The backup provider charges 10× more than expected?

None of these trigger a failover in traditional gateways. Because the response was successfully transmitted — just not correct.

**Correctover** introduces a new category: Verified Failover. Before accepting any failover response, we run it through 6-dimension contract validation:

1. **Structure** — Response format matches schema
2. **Schema** — Required fields exist with correct types  
3. **Latency** — Within SLA range
4. **Cost** — Token consumption within expected range
5. **Identity** — Model field matches what was requested
6. **Integrity** — Semantic quality threshold met

Only when all 6 pass does Correctover accept. Otherwise it rolls back and tries the next provider.

Built on the MAPE-K adaptive loop with 88 evolving self-healing rules and 4 recovery levels. Embedded SDK — your data stays in-process, your own keys, zero markup.
